About me

Hello, I’m Ankitsinh Chavda, a dedicated mobile application developer originally from India and currently based in Canada. As an international student turned professional, I bring a unique perspective and wealth of experience to the world of mobile development. I'm passionate about leveraging technology to create meaningful and impactful solutions that resonate with users worldwide. I completed my Bachelor's degree in Computer Engineering from GTU in India, which paved the way for my career in software development. On this way, I furthered my education in Canada, where I pursued a Post-Graduate Diploma in Mobile Solutions Development at Conestoga College. My time in Canada has not only enriched my academic knowledge but also exposed me to diverse perspectives and experiences that have shaped my approach to development.

Throughout my career, I've had the privilege of working with industry-leading companies and contributing to impactful projects. I began my journey as a Jr. Software Engineer at Corepix Software Solutions, where I gained valuable experience in developing cutting-edge applications and honed my skills in languages like Java, Kotlin, and Dart. Later, I joined Siddhi Infosoft as an Android Developer, further expanding my expertise and collaborating on diverse projects.

Currently, I'm working as an Omni Associate at Walmart in Canada, where I apply my customer-focused approach to deliver exceptional service and support to our customers. My experience in both development and retail environments has equipped me with a unique perspective and a versatile skill set that I bring to every role.

Projects

HandyHelp (React Native with TypeScript)

HandyHelp is a TypeScript and Firebase-developed platform, conceived as a college project, that connects job seekers with those needing assistance for daily tasks. It’s a comprehensive system allowing users to post tasks with details like price, image, and location. The platform supports both professional and general helpers, ensuring the right visibility among job seekers. The chat feature enables detailed task discussions and negotiation, while Firebase manages the backend for fast data transfer. Developers will find the integration of real-time database updates and user authentication particularly engaging, as it provides a responsive and secure user experience.


Reminder (Swift)

Reminder+ is an elegantly designed application, developed using Swift and SwiftUI. It simplifies the process of setting reminders by allowing users to add essential details such as location, image, and time.
Users can personalize their experience by selecting from a variety of ringtones for alarms. Furthermore, the app’s localization feature accommodates a diverse user base with language options including English, German, French, Russian, and Dutch, enhancing accessibility and user engagement.

Tattooist (Java)

Tattooist is an augmented reality app developed in Java for Android, leveraging the OpenCV library. It detects squares drawn on the skin and replaces them with virtual tattoo designs, providing a live preview. For developers, the focus on frame-by-frame image processing and memory optimization is crucial for delivering a realistic and performant user experience.


HAMA (Java)

HAMA is a Java-based online selling platform that merges the storytelling aspect of social media with e-commerce functionality. It allows users to post product images, videos, and descriptions, enhancing the shopping experience. The app includes an in-app chat for direct communication and a story feature to engage the community. Developers will appreciate the challenge of overcoming streaming issues across different devices, which has led to a robust and scalable platform.

Futnet

Futnet is a mobile app that simplifies organizing football matches with friends. It allows for the creation of public or private games, with an easy invitation system. Developers will appreciate the straightforward RSVP functionality and the app’s ability to display public games in the user’s feed, while keeping private matches exclusive to invited friends.

uAlert

uAlert stands out with its Java-built emergency response system that utilizes the phone’s GPS for accurate location tracking. It’s not just an app but a service with real human support, providing 24/7 crisis counseling. Developers will note the two-tier alert system: Medium for notifying the center and emergency contacts, and High for additionally contacting local police. The app also includes features like Check-In, navigation, and health tracking.

GroMart (Flutter)

GroMart is a grocery delivery app built with Flutter, offering a dual interface for customers and vendors. It simplifies online grocery shopping with an intuitive UI and multi-vendor support. Developers will find the comprehensive management system for deliveries, earnings, and orders particularly useful. The app includes a sophisticated shopping cart, real-time order tracking, push notifications, and a secure checkout with multiple payment options.

CabMe (Flutter)

As a developer, you’ll appreciate CabME’s use of Flutter for mobile app development and Laravel/PHP for the web admin panel, ensuring a robust and secure taxi booking solution. The app supports Flutter 3.10.6 and integrates Google Places for setting default locations, offers flexible payment methods, and features automatic video recording for safety alerts. It’s designed to optimize operational efficiency and enhance user satisfaction, providing a seamless booking experience with fare estimation and driver ETA display.

Fitness Guru (Kotlin)

Crafted with Kotlin, Fitness Guru stands at the forefront of home fitness applications. It provides a diverse array of equipment-free workout routines, enabling users to engage in full-body exercises. Designed for individuals aiming to enhance their fitness levels, the app offers a convenient and effective way to pursue health goals. With Kotlin’s robust features, Fitness Guru ensures a seamless user experience, allowing for quick iterations and a stable platform that can evolve with user needs. Whether it’s muscle building, weight loss, or wellness maintenance, Fitness Guru delivers a personalized fitness journey at your fingertips.

Teleprompter (Java)

This app is a comprehensive solution for content creators who require a teleprompter for video recording. Developed with a focus on productivity, the app allows users to read scripts while recording themselves using the front or back-facing camera. It supports script import from TXT, DOCX, DOC, and PDF files, and offers features like text size adjustment, speed control, and audio recording with both in-built and external microphones. For developers, the app’s compatibility with various file formats and its ability to record HD video based on device capabilities are notable. Additionally, the app provides grid display options for positioning, brand logo integration, and a widget-supported interface. It’s designed to reduce recording time and enhance presentation quality without the need for expensive equipment.

Buggy (Java)

Buggy Tech Edition is a Java Android app designed for tech employees in large campus settings. Leveraging geolocation algorithms, it efficiently books the nearest available shuttle within the campus, ensuring prompt pickups. The app checks shuttle space availability before confirming bookings, optimizing employee travel. Exclusive to campus use, it offers real-time updates on shuttle locations and seating availability. Utilizing Firebase authentication for security and Google Maps API for geolocation services, Buggy Tech Edition provides a streamlined, tech-focused transportation solution for campus employees.
